BIKING

Biking, or cycling, is the human-powered activity of riding a bicycle for exercise, transportation, and recreation, offering numerous health benefits like improved cardiovascular health and a longer lifespan, while also providing an eco-friendly and economical way to travel. While it's a low-impact, accessible activity for most ages, potential risks include accidents and overuse injuries, emphasizing the importance of proper gear, bike fit, and safe riding practices.

PARA GLIDING

Paragliding is a recreational and competitive adventure sport where a pilot sits in a harness suspended below a lightweight, foot-launched fabric wing, or "paraglider," which uses air currents to provide lift and allow flight. Pilots can gain altitude by using sources of lift, and the flight controls are simple, consisting of lines used for turning and braking. Beginners often start with tandem flights, flying with an experienced instructor, before progressing to solo flights.

SURFING

Surfing is a water sport where individuals ride moving waves on a surfboard, originating in ancient Polynesia and later popularized in Hawaii. Riders, called surfers, use a surfboard to glide on the forward face of a wave as it moves toward the shore. The sport involves a rich culture, offers physical and mental benefits, and was even introduced as an Olympic sport at the 2020 Tokyo Games.
